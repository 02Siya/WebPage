BhagwatGeeta -As it is



BhagwatGeeta is the epic of India.It is one of the Great, motivational, inspiring book. The Geeta is told by the gretest motivational speaker Shree Krishnaji to one of the great worrior Arjun.There are 18 chapters told by the Shree Krishna, with the help of his guidance Arjun was able to tackle his family members. As Arjun was nervous ti fight with his cousin brothers , but Shree Krishnaji guided him to focus on goal, do your responsibilities and don't care about what will happen or what we will gain in future , just you have to do is your job without expecting anything.   • Fifth Chapter: Karmasanyasyog

  • Eleventh Chapter: Vishwaroop Darshan Yog

  • Eighteenth Chapter: Mokshasanyasyog

Chapter one,introduces the scene of the warland. The Characters, Settings and circumstances involved are described. The scene is the sacred plain of Kurukshetra. The setting is battlefield and the circumstances is war. The main characters of geeta are Supreme Lord Shri Krishna and brave Prince Arjuna. After describing the scenes and characters there has been described the nervousness and feeling of fear of Great worrior Arjuna. It was very difficult for him to battle with their Guru(ideal or teacher), relatives and friends. He was in dilenma how to choose the right path? We also faces such situations in our life that are we on the right path or not?

Chapter two, in this chapter Arjuna telling his dilenma to Lord Krishna and said that it is easy to be a beager and beg for his whole life than firght with his relatives. Even he don't know about the future, that who will win the battle. For this Lord Krishna smiles because he knows everything and replied as, leaving the battleground without fighting is not a sign of great worrior. This was not the expectations from Arjuna, as he was known for his bravery that time. In this chapter , Lord Krishna explained him that relatives, friends or anything that distracts us from our job are all illusion of attachments. They all create a vicious circle and distroys everything.
